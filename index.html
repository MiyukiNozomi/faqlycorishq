My server is only accessible through IPv6.

I cannot guarantee that every single one who accesses this website even has access to the IPv6 internet, and therefore, hosting this page on my server would not only be a pretty bad idea (see answer of question Number 2) but also pointless.

There are a few reasons for that, so lets just go through all of them, one by one:

IPv4 is held together by duct tape and super glue.

You may have head of the term "NAT", stands for Network Address Translation, it's a method of mapping one address space to another. IPv4 can support approximately 4.2 billion unique addresses, which may sound like a large number, but when you realize that there's roughly 11.3 billion devices connected to the internet as of 2021!

One-to-many NAT, the "duct tape".

“The Internet has grown beyond anyone's expectations. Sustained exponential growth continues to introduce new challenges. One challenge is a concern within the community that globally unique address space will be exhausted.”

– RFC-1918, February, 1996.

To free-up more IPv4s, RFC-1918 created what its known as "Private Address Space", or also "Local Network", essentially, now your house and every device on it is represented by a single public IPv4 address. and to make anything on your house accessible to the outside world you'll have to perform what's known as Port Forwarding. Port forwarding, of course, is slower than just directly communicating with the server, but hey, it works!

IPv6, the future of IP.

The Internet Protocol version 6 was originally showcased in 1995 (one year before RFC-1918), unlike IPv4's 32-bit addresses, IPv6 uses 128-bit addresses, technically allowing 2^128 IPs, the actual number is slightly smaller of course as some ranges may be reserved, but hey! that's a number so large that humanity might stop existing before we even exhaust that number of IPs!

Without the need for NATs and port forwarding, the only thing stopping people from acessing your servers and machines is your firewall!

We screwed up.

This idea of private networks was created to be a short term duct tape to buy more time while deploying IPv6, however...

ISPs simply began getting way too good in deploying NATs, to the point that today, ISPs are even placing their clients over 2 NATs! effectively making port forwarding impossible and requiring you to pay extra for a dedicated IP address for your house (which is my case). Nowadays NATs went from a short term duct tape to straight up super glue.

Why LycorisHQ will always remain IPv6-only.

For the future of the internet, and because I'm a poor highschool student LycorisHQ will only be available for the IPv6 internet, even if that means losing possible visitors.
